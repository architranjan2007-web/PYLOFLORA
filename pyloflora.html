The Plant

A Berry Born
in Extreme Conditions

Sea Buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides) thrives in the harshest environments on Earth — from the Tibetan Plateau at 5,200m to the freezing steppes of Siberia. This extreme resilience creates an extraordinary concentration of protective compounds within each tiny berry.

Used in traditional Tibetan, Chinese, and Ayurvedic medicine for over 2,000 years, sea buckthorn was recorded in the 8th-century Tang Dynasty and carried by Genghis Khan's armies. Himalayan cultures called it "Holy Fruit of the Himalayas."

Pyloflora brings this ancient wisdom together with modern extraction science — delivering the complete nutritional profile of the whole berry, nothing added, nothing lost.
